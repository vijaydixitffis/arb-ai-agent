A verb form communicates that something is actively happening. The SA sees "Drafting" in the breadcrumb and understands they are the blocker — not the system. "Draft" reads like a file state; "Drafting" reads like a task.

"agent_review_complete" is a technical implementation name. "review_ready" is what matters to the humans: the dossier is ready and the EA needs to act. The status name should describe the situation, not the machinery that produced it.

Why split "approved" and "conditionally_approved"

These are meaningfully different states for the SA and for post-ARB tracking. "Approved" means done — no further action required. "Conditionally_approved" means the review is published and ARB can proceed, but actions must be closed before the conditional tag is lifted. Conflating them into one status forces the UI to decode the decision field to understand which case applies.

Returned — UX design
  • Status = 'returned' (not 'rework' — more neutral, less alarming)
  • SA receives a structured gap list — not a free-text note — listing exactly which domains and which questions need addressing
  • SA opens the form pre-populated; affected domains highlighted with a "Needs attention" banner
  • SA can re-submit individual domains — partial re-submission allowed
  • Only affected domains re-run by agents on re-submission (not full pipeline)
  • Return count tracked — third return triggers escalation to ARB chair

Deferred — meaning

Deferred means the review cannot proceed at all — not a minor rework but a fundamental gap (e.g. VAPT evidence entirely absent, solution architecture fundamentally misaligned). The review is closed and a new review must be started from scratch after the SA has resolved the underlying issues. Different from 'returned' which is a targeted correction loop.

  • SA notified with full deferral reason and guidance on what to address
  • Review record preserved for audit — not deleted

Why "closed" not "review_complete"

"closed" is a single plain word used universally in issue trackers, ServiceNow, and Jira. It is immediately understood by anyone who has used any workflow tool. "review_complete" reads like a composite internal code. In status badges, "Closed" takes 6 characters vs 15 — cleaner in tables, chips, and notifications.

Seven UX improvements over the earlier design
1 — Progress instead of waitingSA experience
During 'analysing', the SA sees a live 3×3 domain grid. Each cell flips from "Waiting" → "Analysing…" → "✓ Done" via SSE push. This transforms a black-box wait into a visible process. The SA understands what's happening and can estimate when the EA will receive the dossier. Eliminates the "is it stuck?" support ticket.
2 — Partial submission on returnSA experience
When a dossier is 'returned', only the affected domains are re-opened for editing. The SA does not have to re-fill the entire form — the unaffected domain tabs are locked with a green "EA approved" badge. The re-submission triggers agent re-runs only for the changed domains. This turns a potentially demoralising "start over" into a targeted correction.
3 — Action owners need no portal loginAction owner experience
Action owners (often developers, not EA participants) receive a tokenised deep-link that opens a minimal "complete this action" page — no ARB portal account required. They see: what to do, the verification method, a file upload, and a notes field. One page, one action. This removes the biggest friction point in post-approval tracking.
4 — EA "Accept all" shortcutEA experience
For domains the EA agrees with completely, a single "Accept this domain" button accepts all agent findings, scores and ADRs in one click. The EA's attention is drawn to the items that need overriding — which are highlighted in a "Needs attention" sidebar — rather than forcing them to click through every compliant finding. Reduces Gate 2 review time significantly for GREEN domains.
5 — Auto-promotion at closureSystem behaviour
When the last conditional gate action is closed, the system automatically promotes 'conditionally_approved' → 'closed' and notifies the EA. EA has a 48-hour window to reverse this if needed. Previously, EA had to manually flip the review closed — this often caused reviews to linger in a completed-but-not-closed limbo, distorting cycle-time metrics.
6 — SLA visibility everywhereAll actors
Every actor sees their SLA countdown in the header of any page related to their task. EA sees "1d 4h remaining to complete review". Action owner sees "Due in 12 days". At T-4h for EA and T-1d for action owners, the countdown turns amber; at T=0 it turns red and sends an escalation notification. No SLA is invisible.
7 — Inline validation, not a wallSA experience
Gate 1 validation runs per-tab on every Next click, not just on final Submit. Errors appear inline next to the failing element with a one-line fix instruction. Parse failures appear next to the file in the upload tray with a "Re-upload" button. The SA is never shown a list of 12 errors at the bottom of a form — each error surfaces at the point of entry.
8 — Return count escalationGovernance
reviews.return_count is incremented on each 'returned' transition. On the third return, an escalation notification fires to the ARB chair. This surfaces chronic review quality issues without manual oversight. The EA can see the return count badge on any dossier — a dossier returned twice is a signal that SA guidance or the review criteria may need attention.
